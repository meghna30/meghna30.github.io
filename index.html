Meghna Gummadi

Artificial Intelligence Researcher

I am a Doctoral Student in the CIS department at the University of Pennsylvania and a memeber of the Lifelong Machine Learning Group in the GRASP Lab advised by Prof Eric Eaton. My research interests lie in exploring how can we enable AI agents to continually learn and grow through reasoning and exploration. My recent focus has been to learn and exploit representations that enable AI agents to both detect novelty or uncertainty and accommodate it in a seamless fashion, thereby creating learning frameworks that can continually learn throughout their lifetime rendering them safe and reliable for deployment in the real world.

I received my MS in robotics from the University of Pennsylvania and my BS in Electrical Engineering from PES Institute of Technology.
